Historical Article

On 7 December 1941, the Imperial Japanese Navy launched a carefully planned surprise strike on the United States naval base at Pearl Harbor, Oahu, Hawaii.

353 Japanese aircraft — launched from six aircraft carriers — struck in two waves, hitting eight battleships, damaging 188 aircraft, and killing 2,403 Americans. The battleship USS Arizona was destroyed outright.

The following day, President Franklin D. Roosevelt addressed Congress, calling 7 December "a date which will live in infamy". The United States declared war on Japan, entering World War II.
